Health Projects

Change the world by improving public health.



Closing the Disparities in Cancer Program

This program, supported by GSK, focuses on educating and raising awareness about Ovarian and Endometrial Cancer, Multiple Myeloma, and the importance of clinical trails among diverse communities in Los Angeles County. It involved training 21 CSULA public health students A MiOra Health Ambassadors, who then surveyed and educated over 300 community members. The findings were showcased at the 2023 USC School of Pharmacy Moving Targets Conference. Also, two abstracts have been submitted to ASCO2024.

MiOra’s Immigrant Health Literacy & Access Project

In collaboration with Oakland University Master of Public Health students, this project aims to better understand the attitudes, awareness, and behavior of immigrants regarding healthcare and its utilization. Set to continue through the year, the project seeks to identify barriers and opportunities to improve health literacy and access among immigrant populations.

Assessing US Men’s Health Resource Utilization

This project engages with men across the US to understand their knowledge, awareness, attitudes, and behavior of immigrants regarding healthcare and its utilization. Set to continue through the year, the project seeks to identify barriers and opportunities to improve health literacy and access among immigrant populations.

AI Project

With the advent of Artificial Intelligence, this project explores how AI can impact healthcare access and utilization. It investigates the potential changes in the dynamic between patients and physicians brought about by AI. Further, the project aims to understand the opportunities and challenges of integrating AI into healthcare services.

Mental Health Project

Addressing the growing concern of mental health, especially among students, this project focuses on peer-to-peer programs to combat stress and depression. With an estimated 300 million people worldwide suffering from depression, the project assesses the effectiveness and willingness of new university or college graduates to implement peer-to-peer mental health programs.

Healthy Long-Life Project

This initiative aims to promote a healthy and long life through education on vaccines, screening tests, healthy lifestyles, and nutrition. MiOra health ambassadors work to inform community members, leveraging informational graphics and posts on platforms like Instagram to spread awareness.

Policy Project

Partnering with experts in the field, this project educates MiOra health ambassadors on policy and advocacy work.
